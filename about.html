My name is Maab Azem, I’m a 20-year-old software engineer with a clear mission: to make technology simple, logical, and accessible to everyone.

I chose this path knowing it wouldn’t be easy. Many people hear the word “programming” and immediately think it’s something complicated, cold, and impossible to understand — but I’m here to prove the opposite.

Programming isn’t about being a genius. It’s about logic, curiosity, and persistence.

Through my work and projects, I aim to show people that if you can think, you can code. With the right mindset and determination, anyone can turn ideas into real digital products.

I don’t just build websites and applications — I build confidence in people who once thought this world wasn’t for them.

Zero2Code Mission

At Zero2Code, my mission is to break down the barriers that make programming seem intimidating. I want to create a space where beginners feel welcome, supported, and confident to take their first steps into coding.

By providing clear explanations, practical examples, and a structured learning path, I help people transform curiosity into real, usable skills.

Zero2Code is more than just a learning platform – it is a growing community built around the belief that anyone can learn to code. Here, questions are encouraged, mistakes are part of the process, and progress is celebrated.

I believe the best way to learn programming is to build, break, and rebuild. That’s why Zero2Code focuses on real projects that start small and grow step by step – so learners can see progress quickly and stay motivated.

Every topic is explained in a clear, beginner-friendly way, then reinforced with exercises and mini challenges. The goal is not memorizing syntax – it’s learning how to think and solve problems.

Over time, beginners stop feeling lost – and start feeling like creators.
